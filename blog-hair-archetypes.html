Hair Texture Archetypes

by Shambhavi Singh

What straightening my hair taught me about power.

Beyond texture

There's something no one talks about when it comes to hair.

There's something no one talks about when it comes to hair, which is beyond texture. It is identity.

Recently, I straightened my hair after a long period of wearing it naturally curly. It wasn't external pressure but an inner desire trying to tell me something I am about to unpack here.

The two states

Curls as meaning. Straight hair as structure.

🌊
Curls as Meaning

When I wear my curls, I feel deeply connected to something organic. Curls carry symbolism I realised recently. Wearing my natural curls feels like embracing the embedded truth in my DNA. It's expressive and alive — textured in every sense of the word, wrapped up in vulnerability.

  • Softness
  • Romance
  • Divine femininity
  • Emotional openness
  • Freedom
They represent movement and respond to environmental factors such as humidity and friction. Most importantly, they take up space. They are intuitive, and I love that.
✦
Straight Hair as Structure

When I straightened my hair, something else surfaced — a different dimension of femininity that isn't outwardly harder or colder. It signifies a controlled structure that feels like a protective layer in a composed manner.

  • Clarity
  • Sharpness
  • Presence
  • Intentionality
I felt more aware of how I was perceived. More aware of posture and tone. More aware of how my presence lands in a room. It wasn't about looking different but thinking differently. Silent. Contained. Measured.
The myth of authenticity
Reframing the narrative

We're often told that embracing natural texture is the ultimate authenticity — but what if authenticity isn't about choosing one state but knowing when to shift?

My curls are my truth. But straightening them didn't erase that truth. It created contrast, which creates awareness.

With straight hair, I didn't feel less feminine but more guarded — in a way that was intentional. It allowed me to be vulnerable without being exposed, which is the contrasting difference.

"Hair is not superficial. It alters how we perceive ourselves and regulate — and it is so powerful to take that power into our own hands."

— Shambhavi Singh
What buff curl is about

Not about choosing a side.

Buff Curl isn't about "curls are better" or "straight hair is better" or "natural vs styled." It's about understanding the psychological and energetic shifts that hair creates.

✕
"Curls are better."
✕
"Straight hair is better."
✕
"Natural vs styled."

Texture is language, and it communicates something before we speak. When we understand that, we stop styling reactively and start styling intentionally.

My natural curls are embedded in my DNA. That softness is permanent. But the ability to create structure over softness? That's power and awareness. The goal is not to remain one thing but to know what you are amplifying, and why.

The real question
Ask yourself

What version of yourself are you stepping into when you style your hair? Is it one of these?

  • Vulnerability
  • Romance
  • Structure
  • Control
  • Mystery
  • Protection

Hair tools are not just tools. They are instruments of psychological modulation — and that's what Buff Curl explores.
